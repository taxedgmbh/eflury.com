Cookie Policy

Last Updated: October 26, 2024

What Are Cookies? Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website. They help us provide you with a better experience and understand how you use our site.

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how Emanuel Aaron Flury (Taxed GmbH) uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on emanuelflury.com. This policy should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies Always Active

These cookies are necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off. They are usually only set in response to your actions such as setting privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms.

Cookie Name Purpose Duration

2.2 Preference Cookies Optional

These cookies enable the website to remember choices you make (such as your preferred language or the region you're in) and provide enhanced, personalized features.

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often. All information collected by these cookies is aggregated and anonymous.

3. Cookies We Do NOT Use

We do not use:

  • Advertising or marketing cookies
  • Third-party behavioral targeting cookies
  • Social media tracking pixels (beyond basic share buttons)
  • Affiliate tracking cookies

4. How to Control Cookies

4.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data

Browser help resources:

  • Chrome Cookie Settings
  • Firefox Cookie Settings
  • Safari Cookie Settings
  • Edge Cookie Settings

4.2 Impact of Blocking Cookies

If you choose to block cookies:

  • The website will still function for basic browsing
  • Your preferences (like dark mode) won't be saved
  • We won't be able to improve the website based on usage data
  • Some features may not work as expected

6. Local Storage and Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, we use Local Storage (HTML5) to:

  • Store your dark mode preference (key: 'theme')
  • Remember if you've closed certain popups (key: 'newsletterClosed')

Key Differences from Cookies:

  • Local Storage data never expires automatically
  • Data is not sent to the server with every request
  • Larger storage capacity than cookies
  • Can be cleared through browser settings

7. Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. When enabled:

  • We respect DNT signals for analytics cookies
  • Essential cookies remain active (required for functionality)
  • Your preferences are still saved locally
