## The Excel Trap

Most finance teams are stuck in what I call "The Excel Trap":

- **Manual data exports** from multiple systems
- **Hours of formatting** and formula checking
- **Reports that are outdated** by the time they're delivered
- **No drill-down capability** when executives ask questions
- **Version control nightmares** when multiple people edit

Sound familiar? You're not alone. This pattern costs Swiss SMEs an average of **15-25 hours per week** in finance team time. Read our [Power BI case study](/en/case-studies/power-bi-reporting) to see how one firm reduced this to just 2 hours.

## What Power BI Changes

| Task | Excel | Power BI |
|------|-------|----------|
| Monthly P&L | 4 hours | 5 minutes |
| Budget vs Actual | 2 hours | Real-time |

### From Static to Interactive

Instead of 40-page PDF reports that nobody reads completely, Power BI delivers:

- **Interactive dashboards** where users explore their own questions
- **Drill-down capability** from summary to transaction detail
- **Mobile access** so executives can check metrics anywhere
- **Automated alerts** when KPIs breach thresholds

## The Finance-Specific Power BI Stack

Based on implementations across Swiss companies, here's the optimal technology stack:

### Data Sources

- **ERP Systems**: SAP Business One, Abacus, Bexio (via SQL/API)
- **Banking**: Swiss bank feeds via MT940/camt.053
- **Payroll**: Swissdec connections
- **Excel**: Legacy data and budgets

### Data Warehouse

For companies with 7+ data sources, I recommend a lightweight data warehouse:

- **Azure SQL Database** (Swiss data center)
- **Incremental refresh** for large transaction tables
- **Star schema** design for optimal query performance

### Semantic Layer

Power BI's DAX language enables sophisticated calculations:

- Rolling 12-month trends
- Year-over-year comparisons
- Dynamic budget variance explanations
- What-if scenario modeling

## The Top 5 Finance Dashboards

Every finance team should have these dashboards:

### 1. Executive Summary

Single-page overview with:
- Revenue and margin trends
- Cash position
- Top 5 KPIs with status indicators
- Key risks and opportunities

### 2. P&L Analysis

Drill-down P&L with:
- Monthly/quarterly/annual views
- Budget vs actual with variance analysis
- Cost center breakdown
- Trend lines and forecasts

### 3. Cash Flow

Forward-looking cash management:
- 12-week cash forecast
- AR aging with collection probability
- AP payment schedule
- Working capital metrics

### 4. Customer Profitability

Revenue quality analysis:
- Revenue by customer segment
- Customer-level margin
- Concentration risk
- Lifetime value trends

### 5. Operational KPIs

Business driver metrics:
- Inventory turnover
- Days sales outstanding
- Employee productivity
- Capacity utilization

Users can ask questions in plain language:

- "What was our margin by product line last quarter?"

## Implementation Best Practices

### Start Small, Think Big

Don't try to build everything at once:

1. **Week 1-2**: Executive summary dashboard
2. **Week 3-4**: P&L deep dive
3. **Week 5-6**: Cash flow forecasting
4. **Ongoing**: Additional dashboards based on needs

### Design for Adoption

Dashboards nobody uses provide zero value:

- Keep layouts simple and consistent
- Use familiar terminology
- Provide training sessions
- Make mobile access easy
- Gather regular feedback

### Maintain Data Quality

Power BI is only as good as your data:

- Document data sources and transformations
- Implement data validation rules
- Create a data dictionary
- Establish refresh schedules
- Monitor for failures
